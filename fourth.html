Finite Element Methods

The finite element method (FEM) is a popular method for numerically solving differential equations arising in engineering and mathematical modeling.

CAD/CAM

CAD/CAM stands for Computer-Aided Design and Computer-Aided Manufacturing, and it's a technology that combines the two to design and manufacture products.

Power Plant Engineering

Power Plant Engineering is the engineering and technology required for the production of central station electric power.

Heat Transfer

Heat transfer describes the flow of heat due to temperature differences and the subsequent temperature distribution and changes.

Metrology and Instrumentation

Metrology is the study of measurement while instrumentation are devices that measure.

Mechatronics

Mechatronics is a type of engineering that focuses on a combination of mechanical, computer and electrical systems.

Modern Vehicle Technology

The modern automobile is a complex technical system employing subsystems with specific design functions.

Renewable Energy Resources

Renewable energy comes from natural resources that are self-replenishing and can be used to generate energy.

Operations Research

Operations research (OR) is a scientific approach that uses analytical methods to help organizations make better decisions.

Computational Fluid Dynamics

CFD is a branch of fluid mechanics that uses numerical analysis and algorithms to study and solve fluid flow problems.

Nano Technology

Nanotechnology is the study and use of techniques to manipulate matter at the atomic and molecular scale, usually less than 100 nanometers in size.

Additive Manufacturing

Additive manufacturing, also known as 3D printing, is a technology that creates objects by adding material in layers to build up a final product.
